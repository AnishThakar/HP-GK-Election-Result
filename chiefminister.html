Jai Ram Thakur (born 6 January 1965)[1] is an Indian politician, who is the 13th and the current Chief Minister of Himachal Pradesh. He was elected BJP legislative party leader on 24 December 2017 after the defeat of Chief Ministerial candidate of BJP, Prem Kumar Dhumal in the assembly elections held in November 2017. He has been an MLA in Himachal Pradesh Assembly since 1998 and previously served as Cabinet Minister in BJP Government of Himachal Pradesh. He was Minister of Rural Development and Panchayati Raj from 2009-2012. He is elected to Himachal Pradesh Assembly from Seraj in Mandi. He won his first election in the year of 1998 from now delimited constituency of Chachiot (Seraj).

Thakur is married to ABVP colleague Sadhana Thakur and they have two daughters.[6] Sadhana is a doctor, born to a Kannadiga family in Jaipur and brought up there. The couple have two daughters. Both daughters are studying in medical college in Himachal pradesh.
